Our mission

Who VYVE is and why partners matter.

VYVE Health is a Community Interest Company on a mission to make proactive wellbeing part of everyday life — building physical, mental and social health before it breaks, not after. Help yourself. Help others. Change the world.

Our members get live sessions, workouts, AI coaching, nutrition and community in one app — and every 30 activities they log donates a free month of wellbeing to someone in need through our charity partners. Partners are how we bring real, human expertise into that ecosystem.

Why partner with VYVE

What you get as a VYVE partner.

50% revenue share

Earn half of the subscription revenue from every member who joins through you — paid monthly, tracked automatically.

Your own space in the app

We run the platform

Payments, hosting, scheduling, streaming and member management are all handled for you. You focus on your craft.

Enterprise reach

Vetted partner status means we can put your name in front of individual members and enterprise clients with confidence.

Social impact built in

As a CIC, VYVE turns member activity into donated memberships for people in need — your work directly funds access for others.

Grow with us

Background

(A)  VYVE operates a proactive wellbeing platform and mobile application through which qualified experts provide wellbeing content, programmes and sessions to VYVE’s customers (the “Platform”).

(B)  The Partner wishes to provide such content and sessions to VYVE’s customers via the Platform, and VYVE wishes to engage the Partner, on the terms set out in this Agreement.

IT IS AGREED as follows:

1.  Definitions and interpretation

1.1  In this Agreement, the following definitions apply:

“Additional Sessions” means one-to-one, small-group or other bookable sessions that the Partner offers to Customers in addition to standard Platform content, including coaching, consultations and workshops.

“Commencement Date” means the date of this Agreement, or the date the Partner’s space first goes live on the Platform, whichever is the earlier.

“Confidential Information” means all information of a confidential nature disclosed by one party to the other, including business, technical, commercial, financial and Customer information, and the terms of this Agreement.

“Customer” means any individual or organisation (including employees of any organisation) that accesses, subscribes to, or uses the Platform, however and by whomever introduced.

“Data Protection Laws” means the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018 and all applicable laws relating to the processing of personal data.

“Introduced Customer” means a Customer who is first onboarded to the Platform through the Partner (whether by the Partner’s referral code, link, sign-up attribution or other method recorded by VYVE), as determined by VYVE’s records.

“Intellectual Property Rights” means patents, copyright, database rights, trade marks, design rights, know-how and all other intellectual property rights, whether registered or not, anywhere in the world.

“Submitted Content” means any content the Partner uploads, submits, performs, streams or otherwise makes available on or to the Platform, including videos, audio, live and recorded sessions, programmes, written materials, images and community posts.

“Subscription Revenue” means the subscription fees actually received by VYVE from a Customer for their VYVE membership, net of VAT, refunds, chargebacks and third-party payment-processing fees.

“Term” means the term of this Agreement, as set out in clause 3.

1.2  Clause headings are for convenience only. References to “including” mean “including without limitation”. A reference to a statute includes any amendment to it. The Schedules form part of this Agreement.

2.  Appointment and status

2.1  VYVE appoints the Partner, on a non-exclusive basis, to provide Submitted Content and sessions to Customers via the Platform, and the Partner accepts that appointment, on the terms of this Agreement.

2.2  The appointment is non-exclusive. VYVE may engage any number of other partners, and the Partner may provide services to others, in each case subject to the Partner’s obligations under this Agreement (including clause 6).

2.3  The Partner is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency or employment relationship between the parties. The Partner has no authority to bind VYVE. The Partner is solely responsible for its own taxes, National Insurance and VAT.

3.  Term

3.1  This Agreement begins on the Commencement Date and continues until terminated in accordance with clause 17.

4.  The Platform and VYVE’s role

4.1  VYVE will provide and operate the Platform, including hosting, the Partner’s space, payment processing, promotion to Customers, and reporting, and will use reasonable efforts to make the Platform available.

4.2  VYVE controls the Platform, including its features, presentation, content moderation, Customer subscription pricing and packaging, and may change them at its discretion.

4.3  Submitted Content is subject to VYVE’s review and approval before publication. VYVE may decline, remove or require changes to any content that does not meet its standards or policies.

5.  Partner obligations and quality standards

5.1  The Partner shall provide its content and sessions with reasonable skill and care and to a high professional standard, in accordance with Schedule 1 (Quality and Content Standards) and VYVE’s policies as updated from time to time.

5.2  The Partner shall hold and maintain all qualifications, registrations, competencies and consents necessary to provide its services lawfully and safely, and shall comply with all applicable laws and professional codes, including safeguarding and duty-of-care obligations.

5.3  The Partner shall: deliver scheduled sessions reliably; respond to Customers in good time and with respect; maintain consistency of service; keep its profile and information accurate; and complete VYVE’s onboarding, verification and required assessments before going live.

5.4  The Partner shall not provide medical diagnosis or medical treatment, or any advice outside its qualified scope, and shall signpost Customers to appropriate professional support where relevant. (If partner holds the correct level of qualification & or expertise to diagnose then this is acceptable).

5.5  The Partner is responsible for ensuring it holds any professional registration or cover it considers appropriate for its own activities.

6.  Customers, non-solicitation and non-circumvention

6.1  All Customers are, and shall remain, customers of VYVE. The Partner acquires no right, title or interest in or to any Customer or Customer relationship, regardless of how the Customer was introduced.

6.2  All Customer data is owned and controlled by VYVE. The Partner may access Customer data only through the Platform, only to the extent necessary to provide its services, and strictly in accordance with clause 12. The Partner shall not export, copy, retain or use Customer data outside the Platform.

6.3  During the Term and for 24 months after it ends, the Partner shall not, directly or indirectly, alone or with others:

  1. solicit, entice, induce or encourage any Customer to cease, reduce or not take up use of the Platform;
  2. divert, or attempt to divert, any Customer or potential Customer away from VYVE or the Platform;
  3. provide, or offer or arrange to provide, to any Customer (whom the Partner first engaged with through the Platform) services of a type offered on the Platform, otherwise than through the Platform; or
  4. use any Customer contact details or data obtained through the Platform for any purpose outside the Platform.

6.4  The Partner shall not market or promote its own or any third party’s competing products or services to Customers through the Platform, or using Platform-derived data.

6.5  The Partner acknowledges that the restrictions in this clause 6 are reasonable and necessary to protect VYVE’s legitimate business interests, including its customer relationships, goodwill and Confidential Information. Each restriction is a separate and severable undertaking.

6.6  Nothing in this clause prevents the Partner from continuing to serve a client with whom the Partner had a genuine, documented relationship before that client became a Customer, provided the Partner does not use the Platform or Platform-derived data to do so.

7.  Content and intellectual property

7.1  Co-ownership. Submitted Content shall be jointly and equally owned by VYVE and the Partner. Subject to clause 8, each party may use, reproduce, adapt, modify, publish, distribute, communicate, sublicense and otherwise exploit the Submitted Content, in any media now known or later devised, anywhere in the world, without the consent of, payment to, or accounting to the other party.

7.2  VYVE’s rights. VYVE may distribute, promote, adapt, repurpose, excerpt and sublicense the Submitted Content across the Platform and VYVE’s marketing, social and partner channels as it sees fit.

7.3  Partner’s rights. The Partner may continue to use, reproduce and exploit its own Submitted Content elsewhere, off the Platform, for its own purposes, without restriction under this Agreement.

7.4  Fallback licence. To the extent the joint ownership in clause 7.1 is for any reason ineffective, each party grants the other a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, transferable and sub-licensable licence to use and exploit the Submitted Content to the same effect as set out above.

7.5  Partner warranties. The Partner warrants that it owns or has all rights necessary to grant the rights in this clause 7; that the Submitted Content is original or properly licensed; that it does not infringe any third-party rights or any law; and that the Partner has obtained all necessary consents and releases from any individual appearing in or contributing to the Submitted Content.

7.6  The Partner waives, and shall procure the waiver of, any moral rights in the Submitted Content to the extent permitted by law, and consents to its modification and use as contemplated by this Agreement.

7.7  VYVE owns all Intellectual Property Rights in the Platform and the VYVE name, logo and brand (the “VYVE Marks”). The Partner may use the VYVE Marks only in accordance with VYVE’s brand guidelines and with VYVE’s prior consent. All anonymised and aggregated usage data is owned by VYVE.

8.  Revenue share and payment

8.1  In consideration of the Partner’s services, VYVE will pay the Partner a revenue share equal to 50% of the Subscription Revenue received by VYVE from each Introduced Customer (the “Revenue Share”).

8.2  Lifetime guarantee. The 50% Revenue Share is guaranteed for the lifetime of each Introduced Customer’s membership. It shall continue for as long as that Introduced Customer remains a paying Customer of VYVE, and until that Customer makes their final payment to VYVE — including after this Agreement ends — subject only to clauses 8.6 and 18.3.

8.3  The Revenue Share percentage for an Introduced Customer will not be reduced during that Customer’s lifetime on the Platform; it is fixed at 50% for each Introduced Customer.

8.4  VYVE will provide the Partner with a monthly statement of Revenue Share and will pay the Revenue Share monthly in arrears, within 30 days of the end of each calendar month, to the account notified by the Partner. Where the Partner is VAT-registered, the Partner shall issue a valid VAT invoice.

8.5  The Revenue Share is exclusive of VAT, which will be added where applicable. The Partner is responsible for its own taxes arising on the Revenue Share.

8.6  No Revenue Share is payable in respect of amounts not actually received by VYVE, or that are refunded or charged back. VYVE may set off any such amounts against future payments.

9.  No booking fee on one-to-one and Additional Sessions

9.1  VYVE will not charge the Partner any booking fee, commission or percentage on Customers booking one-to-one or Additional Sessions with the Partner.

9.2  This is guaranteed for an initial period of 12 months from the Commencement Date and, in any event, until at least 1 July 2027.

9.3  After that period, VYVE will review this arrangement annually. Any change will be made on not less than 60 days’ prior written notice and will not affect bookings already made. The parties acknowledge this arrangement may need to change from time to time as VYVE develops.

9.4  For the avoidance of doubt, all such sessions are with VYVE Customers and remain subject to clause 6 (Customers, non-solicitation and non-circumvention) and clause 5 (quality standards).

10.  Additional opportunities and projects

10.1  From time to time VYVE may invite the Partner to collaborate on specific projects, including employer workshops, talks, educational content, research and events.

10.2  Any such project, and any additional fees or commercial terms, will be agreed separately in writing (a “Statement of Work”) and, once agreed, will be governed by this Agreement. Neither party is obliged to enter into any such project.

10.3  The parties anticipate that further commercial opportunities for the Partner may arise as VYVE grows, and will discuss these in good faith.

11.  Compliance, safeguarding and conduct

11.1  The Partner shall comply with all applicable laws and with VYVE’s safeguarding, equality, accessibility, content and community policies.

11.2  The Partner shall act with integrity and professionalism at all times and shall not do anything that brings, or is likely to bring, VYVE, the Platform, its Customers or its mission into disrepute.

11.3  The Partner shall promptly notify VYVE of any matter that may affect its suitability to provide services, including the loss of any qualification or registration, any safeguarding or criminal matter, or any material complaint.

12.  Data protection

12.1  Each party shall comply with the Data Protection Laws. VYVE is the controller of Customer personal data.

12.2  The Partner shall access Customer personal data only through the Platform, only as necessary to provide its services, and only in accordance with VYVE’s instructions and this Agreement. The Partner shall not use Customer personal data for its own purposes or outside the Platform (see clause 6).

12.3  The Partner shall keep personal data secure and shall notify VYVE without undue delay on becoming aware of any actual or suspected personal data breach. The parties shall enter into a separate data processing agreement where required.

13.  Confidentiality

13.1  Each party shall keep the other’s Confidential Information confidential, use it only for the purposes of this Agreement, and not disclose it except to those who need to know it and are bound by equivalent obligations, or where required by law. This clause survives termination.

14.  Reputation and publicity

14.1  The Partner shall not make any public statement, or issue any publicity, about VYVE or the relationship without VYVE’s prior written consent, and shall not use VYVE’s name or the VYVE Marks except as permitted under clause 7.7.

15.  Warranties and indemnity

15.1  Each party warrants that it has the authority to enter into and perform this Agreement.

15.2  The Partner warrants that it will provide its services in accordance with clause 5 and that the warranties in clause 7.5 are true.

15.3  The Partner shall indemnify VYVE against all losses, damages, costs and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising out of or in connection with: (a) any breach of clause 6 (non-solicitation), clause 7 (content and IP) or clause 12 (data protection); (b) any claim that the Submitted Content infringes a third party’s rights; (c) any breach of law or of the Partner’s professional obligations; and (d) any claim by a Customer or third party arising from the Partner’s acts, omissions, content or services.

16.  Limitation of liability

16.1  Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes either party’s liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, for fraud, or for any liability that cannot lawfully be limited or excluded.

16.2  Subject to clause 16.1, neither party is liable for any indirect or consequential loss, or for loss of profit, revenue, goodwill or anticipated savings.

16.3  Subject to clauses 16.1 and 16.4, VYVE’s total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total Revenue Share paid or payable to the Partner in the 12 months before the claim arose.

16.4  The cap in clause 16.3 does not apply to the Partner’s liability under the indemnity in clause 15.3 or for breach of clauses 6, 7, 12 or 13.

17.  Termination

17.1  Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on 60 days’ written notice.

17.2  VYVE may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the Partner:

  1. does, or omits to do, anything that brings, or is in VYVE’s reasonable opinion likely to bring, VYVE, the Platform, its Customers, its brand or its mission into disrepute;
  2. commits a material breach of this Agreement (including any breach of clauses 5, 6, 7, 11, 12 or 14), or repeatedly fails to meet the quality standards;
  3. loses any qualification or registration, or becomes otherwise unsuitable to provide its services, or is subject to a safeguarding or serious criminal matter; or
  4. becomes insolvent, enters any insolvency process, or ceases to carry on business.

17.3  The Partner may terminate this Agreement if VYVE commits a material breach which it fails to remedy within 30 days of written notice.

17.4  VYVE may suspend the Partner’s access to, and content on, the Platform while it investigates any suspected ground for termination.

18.  Consequences of termination

18.1  On termination, the Partner shall stop providing services through the Platform and VYVE may remove the Partner’s access and forward-facing presence.

18.2  The co-ownership of Submitted Content under clause 7 survives termination. VYVE may continue to use and distribute Submitted Content already provided, and the Partner may continue to use its own Submitted Content elsewhere.

18.3  Survival of Revenue Share. Subject to the next sentence, the Revenue Share in respect of Introduced Customers onboarded before termination shall continue in accordance with clause 8.2 (i.e., for the lifetime of each such Customer, until their final payment to VYVE), notwithstanding termination. Where VYVE terminates under clause 17.2 (including for disrepute, material breach or breach of clause 6), VYVE may, acting reasonably, suspend or cease future Revenue Share.

18.4  Clauses 6 (for the stated period), 7, 8.2 (subject to 18.3), 12, 13, 15, 16, 18 and 20, and any accrued rights and remedies, survive termination.

19.  General

19.1  This Agreement, with its Schedules and any Statement of Work, is the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes any prior arrangement. Any variation must be in writing and signed by both parties.

19.2  No failure or delay in exercising a right is a waiver of it. If any provision is found invalid or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary, or severed, without affecting the rest. The parties intend the restrictions in clause 6 to be enforced to the greatest extent permitted (“blue pencil”).

19.3  VYVE may assign, novate or sub-contract its rights and obligations. The Partner may not do so without VYVE’s prior written consent. The Partner shall perform its services personally / through approved personnel only.

19.4  Neither party is liable for failure caused by events beyond its reasonable control. No term is enforceable by any third party under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999. Notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses (or email addresses) notified by the parties. This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including electronically.

20.  Governing law and jurisdiction

20.1  This Agreement and any dispute arising out of it are governed by the law of England and Wales, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

Schedule 1 — Quality and Content Standards

  • Provide content and sessions with reasonable skill and care, to a high professional standard, and within your area of qualified expertise.
  • Be accurate, evidence-informed and responsible; never present opinion as clinical fact, and signpost to professional support where appropriate.
  • Be inclusive and accessible: use respectful, inclusive language and consider the needs of a diverse Customer base.
  • Be safe: follow safeguarding and duty-of-care obligations, and escalate any safeguarding concern through VYVE’s process.
  • Be reliable: deliver scheduled sessions on time, communicate changes promptly, and respond to Customers in good time.
  • Be on-brand: follow VYVE’s brand and community guidelines and maintain good production quality.
  • Keep your qualifications, registrations and profile current and accurate.

Schedule 2 — Revenue Share and Payment Summary

This Schedule summarises the commercial terms for convenience. In the event of conflict, the body of this Agreement prevails.

  • Revenue Share: 50% of Subscription Revenue from each Introduced Customer.
  • Guarantee: fixed at 50% for the lifetime of each Introduced Customer, until their final payment to VYVE — surviving termination, subject to clause 18.3.
  • Payment: monthly in arrears, within 30 days of month end, against a VYVE statement.
  • Booking fee on 1-2-1 / Additional Sessions: 0% — guaranteed for 12 months and in any event until at least 1 July 2027, reviewed annually thereafter.
  • Additional projects (workshops, talks, content, research, events): separate terms by Statement of Work.
